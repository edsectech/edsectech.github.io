K12 Technology departments are lean and budgets are tight. Few districts have a security professional, much less a team, and most vendors have priced themselves out of the EDU space. Double that with 1:1 student device programs and an explosion of cloud-based services, and the dumpster is primed for a fire. This page is a humble attempt at compiling some lessons learned from trial and error, research, and collaboration with peers. I'm still early in the security journey, so I hope for this page to improve as my posture improves. This site is perpetually under construction as I hopefully continue to add low-cost (usually free) solutions for hardening and protecting systems. To start, the site is dealing primarily with phishing and mail-based attack vectors. As time goes on, I hope to start documenting device hardening, user policies, administrative controls, as well as documenting some adventures with open source SIEM and Phishing platforms.

I'm also taking the opportunity to work on my CSS game. So - if headers or footers are floating in weird ways... oops.


Why start with mail-based attack vectors?

On the average week in my environment:
  • 5.5% of email messages that flow through my mail system are flagged as Phishing. This does not include ones that make it past our mail system's baked-in threat management.
  • 14% of account logins are blocked because they are coming from outside of North America
